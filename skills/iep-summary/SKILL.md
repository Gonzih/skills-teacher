# IEP Goal Summary Writer

## Overview

Write clear, jargon-free summaries of IEP (Individualized Education Program) goals for parents, guardians, and general education staff. Good IEP summaries translate technical special education language into accessible explanations that help families and colleagues understand what a student is working toward and how to support them.

## When to Use

- When a special education teacher or case manager needs to summarize IEP goals for a parent meeting
- When writing progress reports or goal updates to share with families
- When onboarding general education teachers to a student's IEP goals
- When simplifying IEP language for communication home

## Important Notes

- **Never fabricate specific student data** — only use information provided
- **Maintain student privacy** — use the student's name only if provided and appropriate
- **Do not provide legal or medical advice** — refer families to the IEP team for specific decisions
- Keep language strengths-based and positive while being honest about areas of growth

## What to Collect

Gather the following before writing:

- **Student name or identifier**: (Use "your child" or a provided name/pronoun)
- **Grade level and disability category**: (Optional, for context)
- **IEP goals**: The actual goal text (verbatim is ideal; paraphrased is fine)
- **Current performance baseline**: Where the student is starting from
- **Progress data** (if available): Current progress toward each goal
- **Audience**: Parents/guardians, general ed teachers, or both?
- **Purpose**: Annual meeting summary, progress report, introduction packet?

## Output Format

### IEP Goal Summary for [Student Name / "Your Child"]

**Prepared by:** [Teacher/Case Manager Name]
**Date:** [Date]
**Purpose:** [e.g., Annual IEP Meeting Summary / Progress Update]

---

#### About This Summary
This document summarizes [student name / your child]'s current IEP goals in plain language. These goals were developed by the IEP team to support [his/her/their] growth in school. If you have questions, please contact [case manager name/contact].

---

#### Goal [#]: [Goal Area — e.g., Reading Fluency, Math Problem Solving, Social Skills]

**What this goal means:**
[Translate the technical goal language into a clear, friendly explanation of what the student is working toward. 2–3 sentences max.]

**Where [student name / your child] is starting:**
[Baseline performance in plain language — what can they currently do?]

**How progress will be measured:**
[Briefly describe the measurement method in accessible terms — e.g., "We track how many words per minute they read correctly on a reading passage."]

**Current progress:** *(if data available)*
[Describe progress toward the goal in clear terms — e.g., "As of [date], [student] is reading X words per minute, working toward the goal of Y."]

**How you can help at home:**
[1–2 practical, specific suggestions for parents to support this goal outside of school]

---

*(Repeat for each goal)*

---

#### Summary
[1–2 sentence positive closing that acknowledges the student's efforts and the team's commitment to their success.]

---

## Tips

- Avoid special education acronyms (SDI, LRE, FAPE) without explanation
- Use strengths-based language ("is working on," "is building," not "can't" or "fails to")
- Be honest and accurate — do not sugarcoat lack of progress, but frame it constructively
- Shorter is often better for parent summaries; be thorough for staff summaries
- Always offer a point of contact for questions
