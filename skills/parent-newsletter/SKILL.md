# Parent Newsletter Writer

## Overview

Write warm, informative, and engaging classroom newsletters for parents and guardians. Newsletters keep families connected to classroom learning, upcoming events, and ways to support their child at home.

## When to Use

- When a teacher asks to write, create, or draft a parent newsletter
- When asked to summarize what the class has been learning
- When a teacher needs to communicate upcoming events, reminders, or celebrations to families

## What to Collect

Gather the following information before writing:

- **Class/Grade**: What class or grade level is this for?
- **Time period**: Which week, month, or marking period does this cover?
- **Recent learning highlights**: What topics, units, or skills have students been working on?
- **Upcoming events/dates**: Tests, field trips, projects due, school events, holidays?
- **Classroom news or celebrations**: Student achievements, birthdays, class milestones?
- **At-home tips**: Ways parents can support learning at home?
- **Reminders**: Permission slips, supplies needed, schedule changes, etc.?
- **Teacher name and contact info**: (Optional, for the sign-off)
- **Tone preference**: Formal, friendly/casual, or a mix?

## Output Format

Write the newsletter using this structure:

---

**[Class Name] Newsletter**
**[Month/Week] | [Teacher Name]**

---

Dear Families,

[Opening paragraph — warm greeting and 1–2 sentence summary of what a great period it's been, referencing the class or students positively.]

---

**What We've Been Learning**
[2–4 sentences summarizing recent academic content — be specific enough to be meaningful, but accessible to non-experts. Mention the subject areas covered.]

---

**Coming Up**
[Bulleted list of upcoming dates, events, and deadlines:]
- [Date]: [Event]
- [Date]: [Event]

---

**Class News & Celebrations**
[Brief section celebrating student milestones, birthdays, completed projects, or other positive classroom moments. Keep it upbeat and inclusive.]

---

**How You Can Help at Home**
[1–3 practical, specific suggestions for parents to reinforce learning outside school:]
- [Tip 1]
- [Tip 2]

---

**Reminders**
[Bulleted list of logistical reminders — permission slips, supplies, dress code, etc.:]
- [Reminder 1]
- [Reminder 2]

---

As always, please don't hesitate to reach out with any questions or concerns.

Warmly,
[Teacher Name]
[Contact info if provided]

---

## Tips

- Keep the tone warm and inclusive — all families should feel welcomed
- Avoid educational jargon; explain any academic terms briefly
- Be specific about learning content so parents can have meaningful conversations with their child
- Keep it concise — aim for one page or fewer
- Celebrate student effort and growth, not just achievement
- Include clear action items for anything requiring a parent response
