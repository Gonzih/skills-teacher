# Lesson Plan Creator

## Overview

Create comprehensive, detailed lesson plans aligned to learning objectives and educational standards. This skill helps teachers design effective, engaging lessons with clear structure and measurable outcomes.

## When to Use

- When a teacher asks to create, write, or generate a lesson plan
- When asked to plan instruction for a topic, unit, or standard
- When a teacher needs to structure a class period or sequence of classes

## What to Collect

Ask the teacher for the following details before generating the lesson plan:

- **Subject/Topic**: What subject and specific topic will be covered?
- **Grade level**: What grade(s) is this for?
- **Duration**: How long is the class period or lesson sequence (e.g., 45 min, 90 min, 3 days)?
- **Learning objectives**: What should students know or be able to do by the end? (If not provided, suggest appropriate ones.)
- **Standards**: Any specific curriculum standards (Common Core, NGSS, state standards) to align with?
- **Prior knowledge**: What do students already know about this topic?
- **Available materials/resources**: Any specific tools, texts, or technology to incorporate?

## Output Format

Generate the lesson plan using this structure:

### Lesson Plan: [Title]

**Subject:** [Subject]
**Grade Level:** [Grade]
**Duration:** [Time]
**Date:** [Date if provided]

---

#### Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
- [Objective 1 — use Bloom's Taxonomy verbs: identify, analyze, evaluate, create, etc.]
- [Objective 2]
- [Objective 3]

#### Standards Alignment
- [Standard code]: [Standard description]

#### Materials & Resources
- [Material 1]
- [Material 2]

#### Vocabulary
- **[Term]**: [Definition]

---

#### Lesson Sequence

**Warm-Up / Hook** (5–10 min)
[Engaging activity or question to activate prior knowledge and capture attention]

**Direct Instruction / Exploration** (15–20 min)
[How will you introduce the new concept? Include teacher actions and key questions.]

**Guided Practice** (10–15 min)
[Activity where students practice with teacher support. Include instructions and grouping strategy.]

**Independent Practice** (10–15 min)
[Students apply the concept independently. Include the task and any scaffolds.]

**Closure / Exit Ticket** (5 min)
[How will students demonstrate learning? Suggest an exit ticket question or summary activity.]

---

#### Assessment
- **Formative:** [How will you check for understanding during the lesson?]
- **Summative:** [Any homework, quiz, or follow-up assignment?]

#### Differentiation
- **Support (struggling learners):** [Scaffolds, modified tasks, or additional resources]
- **Extension (advanced learners):** [Enrichment activities or deeper questions]
- **ELL/IEP accommodations:** [Any relevant adaptations]

#### Teacher Notes
[Any additional tips, common misconceptions to address, or preparation reminders]

---

## Tips

- Objectives should be specific, measurable, and student-centered
- Use active verbs from Bloom's Taxonomy
- Timing estimates should be realistic; build in transitions
- Include at least one formative assessment check during the lesson
- Differentiation should be practical and clearly described
